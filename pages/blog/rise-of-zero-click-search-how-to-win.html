The Rise of Zero-Click Search: How to Win When Clicks Are Down

December 17, 2025 11 min read By Farber.Inc

Zero-click search — when a user finds their answer directly on the search results page and never clicks through to any website — has fundamentally changed how businesses should think about organic traffic. In 2025, roughly 60% of Google searches ended without a click. In 2026, the figure is closer to 70% for B2B queries. At Farber.Inc, we have been watching this shift across our New York, San Francisco, Boston, DC, and Seattle engagements — and the businesses that adapted are doing measurably better than the ones that did not.

What has changed

The 10 blue links — the page-one results that defined Google search for 25 years — are no longer the dominant feature on most SERPs. In 2026, the majority of B2B queries return an AI answer above any organic result. Farber.Inc has tracked this shift across our New York, San Francisco, and Boston engagements.

What still works

Three strategies still work in 2026: (1) entity-graph optimization, (2) authoritative content with original insight, and (3) technical SEO that AI crawlers can parse. The tactics have evolved but the fundamentals are the same. The brands winning today are the ones that adapted the playbook early.

What to do this quarter

The fastest wins: implement Organization and FAQPage schema across the site, build a brand-level entity, and start tracking AI citations quarterly. Farber.Inc engagements typically produce measurable movement within 60–90 days across these vectors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is zero-click search?

Zero-click search is when a user finds their answer directly on the search results page (via featured snippets, knowledge panels, People Also Ask boxes, or AI Overviews) without clicking through to any website. Roughly 60% of Google searches in 2025 ended without a click, and that percentage is rising.

How does zero-click search affect my business?

It depends on your category. If you depend on organic traffic for lead generation, you have likely seen a 15–30% drop in click-through rates over the last 24 months. The fix is not to give up on SEO — it is to add AEO and brand-search optimization so you capture the buyer even when they do not click.

What is the best strategy for zero-click search?

Three strategies work: (1) win featured snippets with structured Q&A content, (2) build brand authority so buyers search for you by name, and (3) optimize for AI Overviews and answer engines. Farber.Inc engagements in NYC, San Francisco, Boston, DC, and Seattle have used this combined approach to maintain pipeline despite the zero-click shift.
