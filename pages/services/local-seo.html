FAQ

Local SEO Questions We Hear Most Often

The questions marketing directors and CMOs ask before they engage us for local SEO. If yours isn't here, ask us in the contact form above.

What is Local SEO and how is it different from regular SEO?

Local SEO is the discipline of optimizing your business to appear in geographically-targeted search results — the map pack (the Google Maps block that shows for queries like 'plumber near me' or 'best coffee in Brooklyn'), the local finder, and local organic results. Regular SEO targets national or industry-wide queries without geographic intent. Local SEO specifically focuses on Google Business Profile optimization, citation consistency across directories (Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing Places, industry directories), review velocity and quality, proximity signals, and locally-relevant content. For businesses with a physical location or a defined service area, local SEO is typically the highest-ROI form of search marketing because the conversion intent is higher — someone searching 'emergency plumber Atlanta' is ready to call right now, not browse.

How long does Local SEO take to show results?

Local SEO results fall into three time horizons. Quick wins (2-4 weeks): Google Business Profile optimizations, citation submissions to high-authority directories, and review velocity improvements can show impact on local finder visibility almost immediately. Medium-term (60-90 days): Map pack rankings improve as Google processes the citation consistency, review patterns, and on-site local signals we build. Long-term (4-6 months): Sustained map pack dominance for competitive local queries, especially in dense markets like New York or Los Angeles, typically takes 4-6 months of disciplined execution. The good news is that local SEO compounds — once you have the citation foundation and review engine running, the cost of maintaining rankings is much lower than the cost of acquiring them.

Do I need Local SEO if I already rank well on Google organically?

Yes, almost certainly. Organic rankings and map pack rankings are separate systems. You can rank #1 in organic results and still not appear in the map pack, which appears above organic for most local-intent queries and captures 40-60% of clicks. The map pack also appears for voice searches and AI-generated local answers (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT local queries, Perplexity local recommendations). For a multi-location business, local SEO is how you dominate the entire results page — both the map and the organic listings — instead of competing for scraps below the map. We've helped clients rank in the map pack for queries where they had zero organic visibility, because GBP and citations are evaluated differently from traditional SEO factors.

What is Local AEO and why does it matter in 2026?

Local AEO (Answer Engine Optimization for local queries) is the practice of getting your business cited in AI-generated answers to local questions. When someone asks ChatGPT 'what's the best Italian restaurant in the West Village' or uses Google AI Overviews for 'top-rated dentists near downtown Boston', the AI is reading from a different corpus than traditional search — heavily weighted toward Google Business Profile data, review content, local directory citations, and structured entity data. Roughly 30% of local-intent searches in 2026 now return an AI answer before any traditional local pack. If your GBP, citations, and entity signals aren't optimized for AI consumption, you won't be cited — even if your traditional local SEO is perfect. Local AEO is a 2026 discipline, and we treat it as a core component of every local SEO engagement.

How do you handle multi-location Local SEO?

Multi-location local SEO is a different discipline than single-location. Each location needs its own Google Business Profile, its own location-specific landing page on your site, its own citation profile, and its own local link building. NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency is harder to maintain at scale because the risk of a phone number typo or a suite number change creeping into one of 40+ directory listings is high. We build a per-location dashboard, automate the citation monitoring, and run a per-location review velocity program that respects each market's competitive dynamics. The 25 geo pages on our site (atlanta-ga.html, new-york-ny.html, etc.) are an example of the kind of location-specific content infrastructure that ranks for hyperlocal queries and feeds entity authority back to your main domain.
